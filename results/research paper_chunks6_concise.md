# 1. Paper Overview
This study explores the intersection of learner autonomy and Mobile-Assisted Language Learning (MALL) among advanced English as a Foreign Language (EFL) learners. It provides a theoretical synthesis of learner autonomy and investigates how students utilize mobile devices (smartphones and tablets) to support their personal learning goals and needs.

# 2. Problem Statement
While mobile devices offer significant affordances for language learning, students often lack a systematic approach, frequently relying on intuitive or ad hoc methods within the classroom. There is a need to understand how learners exercise autonomy through technology and the role teachers play in guiding the development of critical adaptive skills to navigate these digital tools effectively.

# 3. Proposed Method
The research employs a dual approach:
*   **Theoretical Synthesis:** A review of literature regarding learner autonomy, specifically the frameworks of Littlewood (1996) and Reinders (2011).
*   **Empirical Study:** Semi-structured interviews with 20 advanced English learners, followed by qualitative and quantitative analysis, to examine their actual usage of mobile devices for language acquisition.

# 4. Data and Experimental Setup
*   **Participants:** 20 advanced EFL learners.
*   **Data Collection:** Semi-structured interviews regarding the use of mobile devices for language learning.
*   **Analysis:** Qualitative and quantitative analysis of participant responses alongside a theoretical review of autonomy as a social, contextual, and multidimensional construct.

# 5. Main Results
*   **Conceptualization:** Autonomy is defined as a non-permanent, fluctuating continuum influenced by context, social interaction, and the development of specific skills. Littlewood emphasizes the necessity of both "ability" and "willingness," while Reinders characterizes autonomy as conscious, informed decision-making.
*   **Technological Impact:** Mobile technologies allow learners to bypass traditional classroom constraints, facilitating direct access to target language resources and users.
*   **Learner Behavior:** Advanced learners demonstrate high awareness of mobile devices as tools for personalized learning and information retrieval.
*   **Current Practice:** Despite this awareness, learners’ use of mobile devices in the classroom remains largely intuitive or ad hoc, indicating a potential gap in critical adaptive application.

# 6. Strengths
*   Provides a robust theoretical grounding by synthesizing key definitions of learner autonomy.
*   Addresses the modern necessity of integrating technology into self-directed learning frameworks.
*   Identifies the specific transition of language learning away from traditional, teacher-led environments toward mobile-supported, autonomous access.
